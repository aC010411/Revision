Network Cables for Wired Network Transmission

Twisted Pair Cable

The purpose of this is to reduce electromagnetic interference (EMI) and crosstalk. There are two main types of twisted pair cables:

1. Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) cables
2. Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) cables

Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) Cables

  • Consists of four pairs of wire twisted together
  • Cat 5 cable is 100Mbps
  • Cat 5e cable is 1Gbps
  • Cat 6 cable is 1Gbps
  • Cat 6e cable is 10Gbps

To connect a cable to a device, RJ45 connectors are commonly used for network connection. To increase the coverage of twisted pair cables, we can use repeater to connect them together.

Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) Cable

  • Similar to UTP cables
  • Offer more protection from EMI and crosstalk, but it is more expensive than UTP cables

There are few types of STP cables for example the coaxial cable and the optical fibre cable.

Coaxial Cable

  • Contains a conducting copper wire core surrounded by insulating materials and braided wires that are enclosed by a plastic tube cover
  • Commonly used in computer networks with bus topology

Optic Fibre Cable

It has a high transmission speed and can serve long transmission distances as the amount of data loss is small. It converts between optical signals (for transmission) and electrical signals (for computer use).
